Every year on the first cold morning, the runners come down from the plateau carrying the salt.

It is not actually salt — it is the year's archive, etched crystal in salt-sealed cases — but the old name stuck, and so the relay is called *loon di daud*, the salt run, and children line the terrace roads to watch the memory of the watershed go by at a dead sprint.

Ask an archivist why the copies travel by foot when the towers could send them in a breath, and you will get the patient look they save for this exact question.

*Because a fact that costs nothing to carry,* she will say, *is a fact nobody carries.* The watershed runs its memory through human bodies once a year the way a family retells a story at dinner — not to store it, but to stay the kind of people who know it.
