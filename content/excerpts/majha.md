The pool-keeper's daughter learned the work the way everyone learns it: by carrying the lamp.

At amrit vela the Crescent is ten thousand black mirrors, and the keepers walk the ring-paths with oil lamps held low, reading the water. A still pool throws the flame back whole. A troubled one — a leak, a root, a sorrow somewhere in the lattice — scatters it.

*You are not looking for your reflection,* her mother said. *You are looking for the water's opinion of the night.*

Years later, in another region, people would ask her how Majha kept a million people cool through the furnace months, and she would try to explain that the answer was a lamp, a path walked in the dark, and the belief — older than any of the pumps — that stillness is something you can serve.
