There is a bench in the Bagh at the kilometre marked zero, where the fence's first post once stood. Two plaques face each other across it, one in Gurmukhi, one in Shahmukhi, and they say the same thing, and the joke — every gardener tells it — is that they argue anyway, about pronunciation.

The grapes came first. Some unkillable vine found the wire in the lawless years and used it as a trellis, and a soldier on watch duty photographed it instead of cutting it, and the photograph went where photographs go, which is everywhere.

*Kandiali taar te angoor.* Grapes on the barbed wire.

It took forty more years, two treaties, and a generation that refused to inherit the silence. But the vine had already demonstrated the engineering principle: a fence is just a trellis that hasn't been forgiven yet.
