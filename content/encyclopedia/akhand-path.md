An akhand path is the **unbroken** reading of the entire Guru Granth Sahib — all 1430 angs, recited aloud end to end without pause, by a relay of readers in shifts (traditionally around two hours each), completing in approximately **forty-eight hours**. It is undertaken at occasions of weight: gurpurabs (timed to conclude on the anniversary morning), the blessing of a marriage or a new house, the passing of a family member, a community in crisis or in thanksgiving — the scripture read *whole* as the maximal act of devotion, the Guru given the family's roof and the family's full attention.

Its mechanics are a small institution: readers arranged (family and sangat members where possible — the tradition prefers participation to proxy), the Granth never left unattended, a lamp kept, the reading audible and continuous through the nights; the conclusion — **bhog**, with the final composition, ardas, hukam, and karah prashad — gathers the sangat. The gentler variant, **sahaj path**, is the same complete reading taken at the family's own pace over weeks or months, and many teachers commend it precisely because comprehension can keep up.

That point is the practice's live debate, conducted within the community with the bani's own backing: a reading nobody understands, *purchased* from professional readers and attended only at the bhog, sits poorly with a scripture whose constant refrain is that mechanical recitation is the disease, not the cure. The Rehat Maryada says it plainly — the path is for understanding. At its best, the akhand path is the tradition's deepest immersion: a household living inside the Guru's voice for two days and nights, the whole canon passing through the house like weather.
