The Mul Mantar — the root formula — is the first sentence of the Guru Granth Sahib and the nearest thing Sikhi has to a creed. Every word was chosen by Guru Nanak; the whole is recited millions of times daily; and a Sikh child often learns it before learning to read.

**ੴ (Ik Onkar)** — One. The numeral *1*, written before everything else, so that no commentary can ever make it plural. **Sat Naam** — whose name, whose identity, is Truth. **Karta Purakh** — the creative being: not a watchmaker who left, but creativity itself, present tense. **Nirbhau** — without fear. **Nirvair** — without hatred, without enmity. **Akaal Moorat** — a form beyond time. **Ajooni** — unborn, not subject to incarnation. **Saibhang** — self-existent. **Gur Prasad** — known by the Guru's grace.

For students, two of these words do the most distinctive work. **Nirbhau, nirvair**: the divine has no fear and no enemies — which means fear and enmity can never be holy, never be done in the One's name with the One's approval. Whole centuries of religious violence are ruled out in four syllables. And the opening numeral: because the One is one, humanity is one; the same light in every creature is not a poetic flourish but a logical consequence, and the langar floor is its application.

Notice also what is absent. No name of a deity belonging to any one tradition; no chosen people; no geography. The Mul Mantar describes attributes — truth, creativity, fearlessness, love without object-limits — that a seeker of any background, including the skeptical, can examine against experience. It does not demand prior belief. It states what the tradition has found, and the rest of the 1430 angs are the working-out.
